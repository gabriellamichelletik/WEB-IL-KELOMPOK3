Abstract

Integrated Learning Project, also known as ILP, is a project that provides students of Saint Ursula Junior High School Jakarta with real-world experience in teamwork, creativity, and entrepreneurship. For the 9th graders, students are required to create a booth and organize a bazaar, where various products are developed, marketed, and sold. Through this experience, students were given the opportunity to apply their knowledge from multiple subjects, such as social studies, mathematics, arts, science, and many more. Behind every successful business, there is always hard work and determination. Countless preparations were done to ensure the success of the bazaar. We’ve done everything from planning and building concepts, to carrying out the bazaar and making all the reports needed.

We started off this project by building our store’s concept. After lots of brainstorming and decision making, we came to a conclusion and decided to name our store ‘Wara Wiri’. We focused on things that related to Jawa, a region in Indonesia filled with tradition, culture, and diversity. We also decided that our main focus was to build a store that was welcoming and friendly. Other than that, our store’s concept was also mildly inspired by Indonesia’s local and one-of-a-kind ‘warung’. Our booth mascot, Wiri, a Javanese Eagle, was also chosen.

Next, we planned our products. This was the first challenge we had to face, because we had to consider a lot of things in choosing products. In the end, we figured it out, from the food, beverages, and products. We picked trendy yet practical products, mainly targeting teenagers. The expenses were also carefully considered, and prices were decided based on what seemed fair and attractive to customers. After finalizing our products and prices, we ended up changing a few things and made some updates. We cut back some food products because they weren’t relevant, and the biotechnology product was completely changed. We also did some minor changes to our PPKN product. Later on, we secured our product list, and afterwards sorted out packaging and stock. During this process, we also had to juggle preparations and rehearsals for a show we had to carry out. The show consisted of a musical drama and a dance recital. Afterwards, several food and beverage products were tested, and experiments were conducted for better results.

As for our booth decorations, we executed it according to the original plan. While making the booth, there were some minor challenges, but we went through all of them. First, we had a tight schedule so we had to work really quickly while maintaining the quality of our booth. Second, the paint we originally bought didn’t work as well as we thought it would. But, some parts of it still could be used. Other than that, we also replaced the paint and bought another set of paints. Lights and tulle fabric were also added to enhance our booth.

To ensure and boost our sales, we did promotional campaigns through social media. An Instagram account was created, and various promotional materials were posted, including videos, posters, and Instagram stories. We posted Instagram stories quite consistently to update our customers. Pre-orders were also opened to ensure early sales. We also shared information about our Pre-Order and bazaar through Whatsapp to family and friends. Our promotional activities went quite well, since no major complications were encountered during the promotional activities and booth preparation.

On the day of the bazaar, our team worked collaboratively to prepare the booth. The decorations were set up, and the products were arranged to attract customers. Despite the booth being located at the far end of the venue, it still managed to draw many visitors. Challenges were encountered, such as limited space, unexpected product demand, and running out of ice for beverages. Nevertheless, our team members quickly adapted, and solutions were found to maintain smooth operations. Despite the heat and uncomfortable conditions, we were relieved that it was crowded. Most of the products were sold out before the event concluded. But, we still had 2 products left. A few of our team members ended up strolling around the school area to find customers and to sell out our products. In the end, all our products were sold out. At around 3 o’clock, we started cleaning and tidying up our booth. We took down our booth decorations, put back all the properties, and cleared and cleaned our table and area.

Through this project, valuable lessons in business management, teamwork, and problem-solving were learned. Collaboration was strengthened, and critical thinking was developed as students navigated the complexities of running a business. We also learned that success doesn’t come easily, and we became even more certain that nothing good can come in an instant. We surely also learned that teamwork truly does make the dream work. All of the struggles we have gone through as a team and all of the efforts we had done as a team did not come to a waste.
